Introduction

Oxygen gas has many useful applications and is commonly stored under high pressure in cylinders. As the pressure increases, there is an increasing danger of mechanical failure of the cylinder itself and/or its flow valve which may result in injury or property damage.

As with any gas in a confined space, the pressure (P) will increase as the temperature (T) and/or mass of the gas itself increases; pressure (P) will decrease as the volume (V) of the container increases.

The ideal gas law expresses these relationships: P=nRT/V. Here, n=mass of gas/molecular weight of the gas, and R is the universal gas constant. The ideal gas law is accurate at low pressures and/or high temperatures. A correction factor, the compressibility factor (z), may be used when pressures get high enough: P=znRT; z may be obtained from a compressibility chart.

There is a need for knowing the pressure in oxygen cylinders. Pressure gauges are useful, but are expensive and failure prone. A shiny app has been developed to help ensure safety; the ranges set for the variables are realistic and such that z can be taken as 1 with little error.

Oxygen Pressure Calculation

This following shows the app function evaluated at defaults. The inputs are m=oxygen mass(kg), tc=temperature(deg C) and v=volume(liters). The output is oxygen pressure in atm. The allowed ranges for the variables in the app itself are realistic: 0.25<=m(kg)<=2.5; 0.0<=tc(deg C)<=50.0; 7.5<=v(liters)<=20.0. In this range of variables, z can be taken as 1.0 with little error. Of course, the app could be easily adapted for working with many other gases.

pressure<-function(m,tc,v) (m*1000.0/32.0)*0.0821*(tc+273.0)/v
m<-1.0;tc<-25.0;v<-15.0;print("Oxygen Pressure in Cylinder (atm) =")

## [1] "Oxygen Pressure in Cylinder (atm) ="

pressure(m,tc,v)

## [1] 50.97042

Using the Shiny App

The app itself is simple to use. Easy to follow instructions are provided. They are as follows.

@@@@@@ Instructions: This app calculates the pressure inside a cylinder containing oxygen which might be helpful in maintaining safety. The inputs required are the amount of oxygen in kg, the temperature in deg C and the cylinder volume in liters. The inputs go in the boxes below as indicated. Use the arrow buttons to input the values. Hit the submit button when finished with inputs. The values used in the calculation are displayed on the right as is the calculated pressure. The ideal gas law is used to make the calculation and should be accurate in the ranges allowed for the inputs. @@@@@@

Check Your Understanding

Question

Which of the following would result in increased pressure of a gas in a cylinder?

  1. letting gas slowly escape from the cylinder in a well ventilated space
  2. moving the cylinder from a shaded location to one in full sun on a hot day
  3. squeezing the cylinder, assuming the walls are flexible and strong enough
  4. 2 and 3 are correct
  5. 1,2 and 3 are correct

See ideal gas law.

Correct answer: 2 and 3 are correct. See ideal gas law. Releasing gas reduces the mass of the gas in the cylinder (n=mass/molecular weight of the gas) and lowers pressure. Increasing gas temperature increases gas pressure. Reducing gas volume increases the pressure.
